Example of use

Thank you for your interest in oxdna.org! The following tutorial will walk you through an example of using the server to simulate a DNA origami.

Example files

DNA/RNA structures in an oxDNA simulation are defined by a "configuration" and a "topology". Download these two files to begin:
  • Configuration
  • Topology
These files describe a wireframe DNA origami from this paper in oxDNA format.

Submitting the job

Next, open the "Submit as guest" link in the sidebar in a new tab to go to the job submit page. On that page you will see a button to upload files. Click that button, navigate to where you saved 23.dat and 23.top, select both (ctrl+click) and click "Open".
You will see the design appear in the oxView window below. Take a moment to make sure its the design you mean to submit. Below that are a series of input fields that define a simulation. Since this is an example, we are going to make some modifications from the defaults.
  • Change "Steps" to 1000000
  • Click "View advanced parameters" and change "Print_conf_interval" to 50000
Now click "Submit Job", after a few seconds you will be taken to the job status page.

Analyzing the output

Once the job status has changed to "Completed", it is ready for analysis. Click the job name in the leftmost column to go to the analysis page. On the analysis page you will find a series of options for built-in analysis options. As an example, try clicking on "Compute mean", wait for the status to change to "Completed" and then click "View means+deviations" to see the mean structure and per-nucleotide RMSF during the simulation.
